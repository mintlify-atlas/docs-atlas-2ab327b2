Lyrica is a lightweight Linux desktop lyrics widget that shows synchronized lyrics on your KDE Plasma taskbar. It connects to your music player via MPRIS2, reads local LRC files, or fetches lyrics from NetEase Cloud Music — all with minimal setup and native desktop integration.

## How it works

Lyrica runs a small Rust backend daemon that monitors your music player via the MPRIS2 D-Bus interface. When a song plays, it tries each enabled lyric provider in order until it finds lyrics, then streams synchronized lines to the KDE Plasma plasmoid over a local WebSocket connection.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build and install the plasmoid">
    Clone the repository, install dependencies for your distro, and run the build script to produce a `.plasmoid` package.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the widget to your panel">
    Use `kpackagetool6` to install the plasmoid, then add the **Lyrica** widget to your KDE Plasma panel from the widget picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play music">
    Start any MPRIS2-compatible player. Lyrica automatically detects the song and displays synchronized lyrics on your panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tune to your taste">
    Open the widget settings to choose lyric providers, adjust font size and color, enable translated lyrics, and more.
  </Step>
</Steps>
