Lyrica is a Linux desktop lyrics widget that shows synchronized song lyrics directly on your KDE Plasma panel. Unlike floating overlay tools, Lyrica integrates natively into the panel itself — lyrics appear as a first-class panel element alongside your clock, system tray, and other widgets, with no separate window to manage.

## How it works

Lyrica has two main components that work together over a local WebSocket connection:

- **Rust daemon** — a lightweight background service (actix-web + zbus) that connects to your music player over the MPRIS2 D-Bus interface, fetches lyrics from the configured providers, and serves synchronized lyric lines at `ws://127.0.0.1:15650/ws`.
- **KDE Plasma plasmoid** — a QML widget that connects to the daemon's WebSocket and renders the current lyric line on your panel in real time.

When you add the Lyrica widget to your panel, the plasmoid starts the daemon automatically. From that point on, whenever you play a song in a supported player, lyrics appear on your panel with no manual steps.

## Why Lyrica instead of a floating overlay?

Most desktop lyrics tools display a transparent floating window that sits on top of your other applications. This approach has well-known drawbacks: the window can block content, it doesn't follow your desktop focus, and it breaks when you go fullscreen. Lyrica solves this by living inside the panel:

- Lyrics stay visible without covering application windows.
- The panel widget respects KDE Plasma's panel rules, stacking order, and multi-monitor setup.
- You interact with it through the standard KDE widget settings interface.

## What's supported

Lyrica supports the following lyric providers, tried in the order you configure them until one returns lyrics:

| Provider | Source |
|---|---|
| `Mpris2Text` | Lyric text embedded in MPRIS2 metadata |
| `File` | Local `.lrc` files on disk |
| `YesPlayMusic` | YesPlayMusic desktop app |
| `NeteaseTrackID` | NetEase Cloud Music by track ID |
| `SPlayer` | SPlayer desktop app |
| `FeelUOwnNetease` | feeluown-netease plugin |
| `Netease` | NetEase Cloud Music by song title search |

**Requirements:**

- KDE Plasma 6.0 or later
- A supported Linux distribution (see [installation](/installation) for the full list)
- Any MPRIS2-compatible music player

<Warning>
  Lyrica does not support Flatpak or Snap desktop environments, or NixOS. These environments impose restrictions on relative paths that prevent the plasmoid from loading correctly. Lyrica is MIT-licensed and open source.
</Warning>
