---
title: "Get started with Lyrica"
description: "Learn how to add the Lyrica widget to your KDE Plasma panel and configure it to show synchronized lyrics for your music player in just a few steps."
---

Once you have Lyrica installed, getting lyrics on your panel takes less than a minute. This guide walks you through adding the widget, playing music, and making your first configuration changes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install Lyrica">
    If you haven't installed Lyrica yet, follow the [installation guide](/installation) to build and install the plasmoid on your system.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the widget to your panel">
    Right-click an empty area of your KDE Plasma panel and select **Add Widgets**. In the widget picker, search for **Lyrica**, then drag it onto your panel.

    <Note>
      The Lyrica backend daemon starts automatically as soon as you add the widget to your panel. You don't need to launch it manually.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Play music in an MPRIS2-compatible player">
    Start playing a song in any MPRIS2-compatible music player. Common players that work out of the box include:

    - **Elisa** — KDE's built-in music player
    - **VLC** — with the MPRIS2 plugin enabled
    - **Spotify** — via the official Linux client
    - **Rhythmbox** — with the MPRIS2 plugin enabled

    Lyrica detects the active player automatically through the MPRIS2 D-Bus interface.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Watch lyrics appear on your panel">
    As each lyric line becomes active, Lyrica displays it on your panel in real time. Lyrics scroll with the song — no clicks required.

    <Tip>
      If lyrics don't appear, check that your player is MPRIS2-compatible and that it is not listed in the disabled players section of the widget settings. Some players also need an MPRIS2 plugin enabled (for example, Rhythmbox and VLC).
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure lyric providers and appearance (optional)">
    Click the **Settings** icon on the Lyrica widget to open its configuration panel. From here you can:

    - Enable or disable specific lyric providers and set their fallback order
    - Adjust font size, color, and text alignment
    - Show translated or romanized lyrics alongside the original
    - Manage the list of active and disabled players
  </Step>
</Steps>
